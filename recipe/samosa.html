Samosa

Description

Samosa is a popular Indian snack known for its crispy and flaky pastry filled with a savory mixture of spiced potatoes, peas, and sometimes meat. These triangular pastries are deep-fried to perfection and are often served with chutney. Perfect as an appetizer or a tea-time snack, samosas are a beloved treat in many cultures.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil or ghee
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • 3 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1/2 cup green peas, boiled
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 2 tablespoons fresh coriander, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

Steps

  1. Prepare the dough: In a large bowl, mix the flour, salt, and vegetable oil or ghee. Gradually add water and knead into a firm dough. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the filling: In a pan, heat a tablespoon of oil. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add the chopped onions and green chilies, and saut√© until the onions are golden brown.
  3. Add the mashed potatoes, boiled peas, garam masala, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few minutes. Add chopped coriander leaves and mix. Let the filling cool.
  4. Shape the samosas: Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each portion into a ball. Roll each ball into a thin oval or circle. Cut each circle in half to form two semi-circles.
  5. Form a cone shape with each semi-circle by folding it and sealing the edge with water. Fill the cone with the potato mixture and seal the top edges to form a triangular samosa. Repeat with remaining dough and filling.
  6. Fry the samosas: Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully slide in a few samosas at a time. Fry until golden brown and crispy on all sides. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  7. Serve: Serve hot samosas with your favorite chutney or sauce. Enjoy!
