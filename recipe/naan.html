Naan

Description

Naan is a traditional Indian flatbread that is soft, fluffy, and slightly chewy, often enjoyed with a variety of dishes such as curries and tandoori. Made with a simple dough of flour, yeast, yogurt, and milk, Naan is typically cooked in a tandoor oven, but it can also be easily prepared at home using a skillet. This recipe will guide you through making delicious homemade Naan that is perfect for soaking up rich, flavorful sauces or for eating on its own as a tasty treat.

Ingredients

  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter (for brushing)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)

Steps

  1. Activate the yeast by dissolving the sugar in the warm water, then stirring in the yeast. Let the mixture sit for about 10 minutes, until it becomes frothy.
  2. Combine the flour, yogurt, milk, salt, and vegetable oil in a large mixing bowl. Add the yeast mixture and knead the dough until smooth and elastic, about 6-8 minutes.
  3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until it has doubled in size.
  4. Divide the dough into 8 equal portions. Roll each portion into a ball, then flatten each ball into a round or oval shape about 1/4 inch thick.
  5. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat. If desired, mix the melted butter with the minced garlic for extra flavor.
  6. Cook the naan in the hot skillet, one at a time. Cook for 2-3 minutes on one side, until bubbles form and the bottom is golden brown. Flip and cook for another 2 minutes on the other side.
  7. Brush the cooked naan with melted butter (and garlic, if using) and sprinkle with fresh cilantro if desired. Serve warm.
